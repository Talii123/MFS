My Fibrolamellar Story

This project is about helping patients share their stories with others and gathering information to help our community.

At the center of it all is an interactive timeline. On the right, you can see events categorized by type - surgeries, chemos, diagnoses etc. If you click on the title of the event, it will take you to the event’s location on the timeline. Below the timeline is a section for the patient to tell their story in detail. You can click on the titles on the left and the chapter will be visible in the story box and highlighted on the timeline.

This prototype is “based on a true story,” but it’s not 100% accurate. The treatments, side effects and results are accurate. The dates may be wrong, but the order of events and their durations are correct.

I’m using aliases for the doctors' names and the hospitals. The reason for this is that the prototype you’re viewing is LIVE on the Internet- it’s public so anyone can see it. I’ve decided (for now) to keep a few things private.

Hope you enjoy it!
Tal

Show Surgeries on Timeline?

  • Liver Resection

    Performed: November 29, 2006
    Surgeon: Dr. Melissa Newton
    Hospital: A Metro Hospital
    Location: Toronto, ON, Canada
    Recovery time in hospital: 7 days
    Recovery time at home: about 3 months

    Description:

    My surgical oncologist, Dr. Newton, performed an 8 hour surgery. She removed 60% of my liver, and also took out my gall bladder and all of the tubing connecting the liver and gall bladder to the other organs. She then surgically attached the leftover liver to my intestines. She also cut out a piece of my diaphragm because the liver tumor was abutting it. Finally, she took out the tube that they put in my abdomen earlier to bypass the blocked bile duct and allow the bile to drain.

    Finally, she removed three lymph nodes, which were all negative (no cancer in them).

    She told me that she got all of the cancer out and that hopefully it didn't have a chance to spread before the surgery.


  • Diaphragm & Lymph Nodes Resection

    Performed: December 15, 2007
    Surgeon: Dr. Tremblay
    Hospital: M.D. Anderson Cancer Center
    Location: Houston, TX, USA
    Recovery time in hospital: 7 days
    Recovery time at home: 2 weeks

    Description:

    Dr. Tremblay performed an 8 and a 1/2 hour surgery. He removed an enlarged lymph node from my diaphragm, then cut a hole in my diaphragm so that he could get into my chest cavity where the other tumor was. He then removed a mass from my chest.

    He used the same incision site as last time so there is no new scar. He also used a glue to close up the wound instead of staples, so the scar was much thinner.


  • Lymph Nodes Resection

    Performed: December 17, 2009
    Surgeon: Dr. Tremblay
    Hospital: M.D. Anderson Cancer Center
    Location: Houston, TX, USA
    Recovery time in hospital: 7 days
    Recovery time at home: 2 and a half weeks

    Description:

    This surgery required two surgeons. First, one surgeon removed the lymph nodes from in front of my heart. Then, Dr. Tremblay came in to do the abdominal surgery. Because Dr. Tremblay knew from the previous surgery about the scarring around my pancreas, he planned a different route to reach the suspicious lymph nodes and was able to remove them all. Before closing me up, he did an ultrasound directly on my liver, which showed no sign of disease.

  • Nexavar (Sorafenib)

    Started: October 20, 2007
    Ended: October 30, 2007
    Duration: 10 days
    Oncologist: -- censored --
    Result: Progressive Disease

    Side Effects:

    1. Full Body Rash
    2. Extreme Swelling of the Feet
    3. Pain When Standing
    4. Elevated Red Blood Cell Counts
    5. Green Growths on My Fingers
    6. Hair Loss
    7. Fatigue

    Do I Recommend this Treatment? NO

    Description:

    I tried Nexavar for 10 days but had really bad side effects.

    For a week I had terrible stomach cramps. When that finally went away, I got a full body rash, my feet were so swollen I could barely get my shoes on and it hurt like hell to stand up. Worst of all, my red blood cell counts were way off and I had to see a hematologist to make sure I wasn't in any danger from the chemo.

    It took 2 weeks of prednisone for me to recover. The medical oncologist wanted to try again at 1/8 the original dose, but I wanted to try a different treatment.


  • 5FU and Interferon Alpha

    Started: January 28, 2008
    Ended: December 8, 2008
    Duration: 9 months
    Oncologist: -- censored --
    Result: Partial Response

    Side Effects:

    1. Extreme Fatigue
    2. Mouth Sores
    3. Thrush
    4. Muscle Aches
    5. Headaches
    6. Difficulty Thinking/Focusing
    7. Elevated Heart Rate (at the beginning of rounds 1 and 2 only)

    Do I Recommend this Treatment? YES

    Description:

    Cycles would last 3 weeks, with one week off between them.

    The 5FU was administered with a bottle around the size of a baby bottle full of 5FU, which would slowly infuse into the body over the course of the week. At the end of each week, the empty bottle was replaced with a full one. Three bottles over three weeks would constitute one round.

    The Interferon Alpha is administered with injections into fatty tissue - either in the belly or the thigh.


  • Xeloda and Interferon Alpha

    Started: February 1, 2010
    Ended: May 31, 2010
    Duration: 3 months
    Oncologist: -- censored --
    Result: unknown

    Side Effects:

    1. Swollen Hands and Feet
    2. Hot Feeling in Hands and Feet
    3. Elevated Heart Rate (only in the beginning)


    Description:

    Xeloda (Capecitabine) is a very similar drug to 5FU, except it is delivered in a pill form, while 5FU is given intravenously. Xeloda actually gets turned into 5FU by the human body.

    I did this chemo as a precaution. There was no evidence of disease, but because I recently had cancer growing in my body, we were concerned that there could still be cancerous cells hiding in my blood, lymph, or some other tissues.

    We figured that since the 5FU & Interferon worked so well in the past, I should do some additional chemo now.

    I opted for Xeloda this time because of quality of life issues - it's much more convenient to take Xeloda pills than to have a bottle of 5FU attached to your PICC line at all times. Although we heard some people say that 5FU works better than Xeloda in this disease, we figured that since this was just preventative chemo anyway, my quality of life should count for more. If I had active disease, I probably would have gone with 5FU not Xeloda.

  • Unmatched Donor Lymphocyte Infusions

    Started: December 9, 2008
    Ended: February 28, 2009
    Duration: 3 months
    Result: Stable Disease

    Description:

    Injected with T-cells and Natural Killer cells from various unmatched donors. Small non-chemotherapeutic doses of Avastin and Erbitux were administered to enhance the donated cells' ability to target the tumors.


  • Three different cancer cell vaccines administered.

    Started: April 9, 2009
    Ended: June 28, 2009
    Duration: 3 months
    Result: Stable Disease

    Description:

    Three different cancer 'vaccines' were prepared and administered. White blood cells were taken from my brother, who is an 80% match for me.

    Vaccine #1: My brother's T-cells and Natural Killer cells were mixed with my frozen tumor in a lab and then infused into me, along with IL2, Avastin and Erbitux.

    Vaccine #2: My brother's dendritic cells were exposed to my frozen tumor and then infused into me along with IL2, Avastin and Erbitux.

    Vaccine #3: My own T-cells were extracted and mixed with my brother's dendritic cells and my frozen tumor in the lab, and then infused into my body.

In My Own Words:

I was diagnosed in September 2006 at the age of 25. I was very lucky. The tumor blocked my bile duct which made me more yellow than Bart Simpson, so we caught it early.

Two painful procedures and an 8hr surgery later and the doctors told me to get on with my life.

In September 2007, it came back. They spotted two tumors - one in the chest and one in my diaphragm. I also had enlarged lymph nodes. The only thing my doctors wanted to try was Sorafenib ("Nexavar"). After 10 days, I had to stop - it was killing me faster than the cancer was. My doctors wanted to give up, but my family didn't, so we found other doctors.

Eventually I had a surgery to remove the tumors. They couldn't safely remove the lymph nodes so they gave me some radiation during the surgery. After that, I did 9 rounds of chemotherapy - 5FU and Interferon Alpha. The lymph nodes shrunk somewhat after the first 3 rounds and stayed stable after that. I then went and tried some cell therapy (like Sarah) and the nodes continued to be stable.

In October 2009, the lymph nodes started growing again. I had surgery for a 3rd time, and this time, they got it all. I did 3 more rounds of chemo as a precaution, and now I have no evidence of disease.
